ABOUT POCKET_PHP
Years of experience of freelancing as software developer has made me weary of web related jobs for a variety of reasons, but primarily due to how unreasonably common it has become for even the most trivial of websites to be built on top of bloated frameworks that do very little to solve their native language problems and only expand on trendy designs.

The result is a complex, slow and semi-independent codebase that isn't merited on the vast majority of the cases. While it's usually easy to critize such terrible engineering cecisions the truth is that the available languages to script web servers all sucffer from their own, respective ills. PHP, though it has changed for the better it still suffers from (mostly legacy) issues that makes it relatively dagnerous for new programmers to use in production environments. Pocket_php tries to find a middle points between fixing some of PHP's more serious isses and becoming the generic, over engineered framework.

Pocket_php requires no extra libraries, uses no first, third party (or any) package managers, works with all modern webservers and adds virtually no overhead while providing the eternal, cookie cutter tools a website needs like HTML templating and session management. Enjoy!
